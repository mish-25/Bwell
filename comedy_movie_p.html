COMEDY MOVIES FOR KIDS!

1.THE PINK PANTHER


The special Pink Panther diamond has gone missing, and the Chief Inspector has a plan: Put his best detective on the case while the quirky Inspector Jacques Clouseau "solves" it publicly. Clouseau messes up a lot, his blunders actually help him solve the case.

2.THE SECRET LIFE OF PETS


Max is a dog who loves his sweet life, but when his owner brings home a giant, mean canine named Duke, they both know they're in for trouble. The duo mistakenly ends up in a truck to the pound, so their group of pals goes on a mission to find them on the streets of a big city.

3.KICKING AND SCREAMING


Buck always wanted his son, Phil, to be an athlete — it just didn't work out that way. So when grown-up Phil becomes his son's soccer coach, he wants to turn their last-place team into champs so they can beat Buck's first-place winners.

4. FERRIS BEULLER'S DAY OFF


Ferris Bueller knows how to cut class and get away with it, so he's determined to have one last go-around before graduation. He takes his friends with him through the streets of Chicago — but his high school principal Rooney is adamant about catching him in the act so he can suspend Bueller once and for all.

5.MRS DOUBTFIRE


Daniel Hillard is a worried he'll lose his connection with his kids after he divorces his wife. So, with the help of his brother, Daniel dresses as a British woman and tricks his ex-wife to hire him as their nanny. He must win over the children as his alter ego "Mrs. Doubtfire" — but not everything goes as planned.

6.ELF


In a strange mix of events, Buddy the human was accidentally sent to the North Pole as a toddler and raised as an elf. As an adult, Buddy ventures to New York City to find his real father — who just happens to be a mean businessman who hates Christmas. You can watch this film all year long.

7.HOME ALONE


Home Alone is a 1990 American comedy blockbuster film directed by Chris Columbus written and produced by John Hughes and distributed by 20th Century Studios. It was followed by two theatrical sequels, two made-for-television films, and a reboot. Kevin McCallister (Macaulay Culkin), an eight-year-old boy whose wish of his family to disappear comes true when they mistakenly left him at home when they go on a vacation to Paris for Christmas. While initially relishing time by himself, he is later greeted by two burglars, Harry and Marv, who are played by Joe Pesci and Daniel Stern, respectively. Kevin eventually manages to outwit them with a series of booby traps.

8.SHREK


Shrek is a mean ogre who loves his isolated swamp. But when his home is invaded by fairy-tale characters banished by Lord Farquaad, he must save their homes by rescuing Princess Fiona, so she can be the Lord's bride.
